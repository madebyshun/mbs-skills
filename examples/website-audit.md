# Example — website-audit

## Input

> Audit this website. It's a portfolio site for a designer — homepage with a hero section, project grid, about section, and contact form. The hero says "I design things." Navigation is minimal: just Home, Work, About. No mobile optimization done yet.

## Output

**Structure & Navigation — 6/10**
Clean and minimal, but "I design things" tells me nothing. What kind of things? For whom? The nav works but there's no clear path — a recruiter and a potential client would need different journeys. Add a short descriptor in the hero and consider a "Hire me" or "Get in touch" in the nav for direct intent.

**Visual Design — 8/10**
Strong if the project grid carries good work. The minimal approach works for a portfolio. Loses points without seeing actual execution — whitespace, typographic rhythm, and image cropping will make or break this.

**Copy Clarity — 4/10**
"I design things" is the weakest possible headline. It's trying to be clever but ends up vague. Replace with something specific: who you design for, what outcomes you create, or what your POV is. The about section needs to answer "why hire this person?" not just list tools.

**CTA Effectiveness — 5/10**
Contact form at the bottom is passive. Users who are interested will look, but you're not guiding anyone there. Add a CTA button in the hero, and consider a sticky "Let's work together" link in the nav.

**Mobile Experience — 3/10**
No mobile optimization is a hard deduction. Over 60% of portfolio traffic comes from mobile (people checking on their phone after meeting you). Fix this before sending the link to anyone.

---

**Top priority:** Mobile optimization, then rewrite the hero headline.
