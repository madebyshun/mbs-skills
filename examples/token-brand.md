# Example — token-brand

## Input

> Review this token brand. It's called GRIZ — a community memecoin on Base built around a grizzly bear mascot. The bear is drawn in a flat, bold cartoon style. Brand colors are burnt orange and dark brown. The narrative is "hibernation to accumulation" — bears that hold through the winter get rewarded in spring. Launched with no VC, fair launch, community-driven.

## Output

**Name Memorability — 9/10**
GRIZ is excellent. Short, punchy, easy to spell, unambiguous in all caps. It has a physical feel — you can almost hear it. Works as a ticker, a community noun ("I'm a GRIZ"), and a verb ("grizbrained"). This is rare.

**Visual Identity — 7/10**
Flat bold cartoon style is the right call for a memecoin — it prints well on merch, reads fast in small sizes, and gives the community something to avatar. Burnt orange and dark brown is a distinctive palette in a sea of green frogs and gold dogs. The risk: it can read as rustic/earthy rather than crypto-native. Add one electric accent color (a bright orange or off-white) to give it energy on dark backgrounds.

**Community Vibe — 8/10**
Fair launch + no VC is a strong narrative in the current meta. The community will trust it more. "We are all GRIZ" practically writes itself. The mascot gives people something to identify with — this is the mechanic behind every successful memecoin community.

**Narrative / Story — 9/10**
"Hibernation to accumulation" is genuinely smart. It reframes holding as an identity, maps perfectly to the bear mascot, and creates a seasonal content calendar. Spring pump narrative will write itself. This is the strongest asset of this brand.

**Differentiation**
Bear memecoins exist (BERACHAIN adjacent energy, various bear tokens) but GRIZ's narrative specificity sets it apart. The hibernation arc is ownable. Most bear tokens just use the animal — you've built a lore around behavior.

---

**Would I hold the bag?** Yes — the name, narrative, and no-VC positioning are all working together. The main risk is execution: community needs consistent content feeding the hibernation arc to keep the story alive.
