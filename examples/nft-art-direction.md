# Example — nft-art-direction

## Input

> Review this NFT collection's art direction. It's a 5,000 piece generative PFP collection called "Void Runners" — characters set in a post-apocalyptic neon city. Base character is a hooded figure. Traits include: hood color (15 options), visor type (10 options), background environment (8 options), and accessories like weapons and gadgets (20+ options). The art style is pixel art at 48x48 upscaled to 1000x1000.

## Output

**Visual Consistency — 8/10**
Pixel art at 48x48 is a smart constraint — it forces consistency across all trait combinations. Upscaling to 1000x1000 works if done with nearest-neighbor (no blurring). The hooded figure silhouette gives every piece a recognizable shape at thumbnail size, which is critical for PFP use.

**Trait Design — 6/10**
The trait count is solid but the distribution raises questions. 20+ accessories is a lot — with 5,000 pieces, many accessories will appear so rarely they lose meaning, while others will feel common. Audit your rarity curve: the sweet spot is accessories that feel special at 2–5% but are numerous enough that communities form around them.

**Rarity Feel — 5/10**
No legendary or 1/1 tier mentioned. At 5,000 pieces, you need at least one tier that feels genuinely rare and visually distinct — a completely different background treatment, an animated trait, or a collaboration piece. Without this, the top of the rarity curve is just "slightly more accessories."

**Storytelling**
"Void Runners" and post-apocalyptic neon city is a strong world. The hooded figure has lore potential. What are they running from? The name implies movement and purpose — lean into this. Each background environment should feel like a location in a larger world, not just a backdrop.

**Collector Appeal — 7/10**
PFP utility depends on the silhouette reading well at 400x400 (Twitter) and 200x200 (Discord). The hooded figure likely works. The neon-on-dark palette will look great in dark mode feeds. Risk: pixel art PFPs are crowded — the differentiation has to come from lore and community, not just the art.

---

**To make this more desirable:** Define the lore more sharply, add a visually distinct legendary tier, and audit the accessory rarity curve before mint.
