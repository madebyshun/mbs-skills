# Example — brand-naming

## Input

> Help me name this brand. It's a mobile app for tracking personal finances with a focus on simplicity — no budgeting categories, no complicated charts. Just one number: your "spending runway" — how many days you can spend at your current rate before you need to worry. Target audience is young professionals who find traditional finance apps overwhelming.

## Output

**1. Runway** *(literal)*
Exactly what the app measures. Clean, short, already understood by your audience. Risk: used broadly in startup culture. Best as a working name.

**2. Clearway** *(metaphorical)*
The clear path ahead. Implies freedom and visibility without financial jargon. Soft and approachable.

**3. Keel** *(abstract)*
Nautical — the part of a ship that keeps it stable. Implies balance without being obvious about it. Memorable, short, unusual in fintech.

**4. Dayspan** *(literal/invented)*
Directly references the "days" mechanic of the app. Functional and descriptive. Could work as a fintech brand with the right visual treatment.

**5. Luma** *(abstract)*
Light, clarity, visibility. Works for a finance product that simplifies. Risk: very popular name space across apps.

**6. Floatly** *(invented)*
Playful take on "staying afloat." The -ly suffix reads as modern app naming. Approachable for an audience that finds finance stressful.

**7. Headroom** *(metaphorical)*
Space above you — financial breathing room. Instantly communicates the core value. Slightly longer but very resonant.

**8. Ovr** *(abstract/invented)*
Short for "overview." Minimal, modern, easy to say. Works well as an app icon wordmark.

**9. Nomly** *(invented)*
Riff on "economy" — friendly, invented, ownable. Low trademark conflict risk.

**10. Spendline** *(literal)*
Combines spend + timeline + lifeline. Says exactly what it does. Less glamorous but highly searchable.

---

**Top picks:** Keel (most memorable, distinct in fintech), Headroom (strongest concept match), Runway (clearest communication). All three are worth trademark checking.
