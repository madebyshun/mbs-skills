# Example — landing-page-review

## Input

> Review this landing page. It's for a SaaS tool that generates social media captions with AI. Headline: "Write better captions, faster." There's a product screenshot below the fold, 3 pricing tiers, and a "Start free trial" CTA button. No testimonials. Email signup at the bottom.

## Output

**Headline Strength — 7/10**
"Write better captions, faster" is solid — clear benefit, addresses both quality and speed. But it's also safe and forgettable. Who is this for? A social media manager, a solo creator, a brand team? Specificity increases conversion. Test: "The AI caption writer for creators who post daily."

**Above-the-Fold Clarity — 6/10**
If the product screenshot is below the fold, users are making a decision before seeing what they're buying. Move a cropped, compelling UI screenshot or a short animated demo above the fold. The first screen should answer: what is this, who is it for, and why should I care.

**Value Prop**
The headline covers speed and quality but misses the emotional angle — the frustration of staring at a blank caption box. Lead with the pain: "Never stare at a blank caption again."

**Social Proof — 3/10**
No testimonials is a significant trust gap. Even 2–3 short quotes with a name and role would materially increase conversions. If you don't have them yet, add a "Join X creators" user count or logos of brands using the tool.

**CTA — 7/10**
"Start free trial" is strong — removes commitment friction. Make sure it's above the fold and repeated at least once mid-page. The email signup at the bottom is a second-tier CTA that's fine as a fallback.

---

**First change to make:** Add social proof. A single real testimonial will outperform any copy tweak.
